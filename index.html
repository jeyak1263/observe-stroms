Severe Storms: Tornadoes

The weather can change from one minute to the next. At this very moment on Earth there are roughly two thousand thunderstorms raining down around the world.

Thunderstorms form when warm, moist air rises and meets colder air in Earth‚Äôs atmosphere. As the warm air cools, moisture turns into rain. This rising and falling cycle is called a storm cell.

Sometimes, if conditions are right, these storms can make tornadoes. A tornado is a fast-spinning column of air that forms out of a thunderstorm.

People cannot stop tornadoes from forming. But they can learn to predict them and prepare to stay safe.
